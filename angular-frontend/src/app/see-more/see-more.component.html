TELECOMMUNICATIONS SECURITY

Telecommunication can be defined as the transmission, emission or reception of information by any electromagnetic system. Telecommunications therefore allow rapid exchanges between people. These exchanges may relate to private life data. Therefore, the security of telecommunications must be ensured. Telecommunications security will prevent intrusions into the exchange circuit and will protect the privacy of individuals.

Future of telecommunications

Satellite communications underpin virtually every aspect of our daily lives. One of the key areas of innovation is the integration of space assets into preparations for 5G, to facilitate the realization of the promised global connectivity. 5G will provide several new business opportunities in the “Internet of Things”, connectivity in transportation and logistics, automated media and broadcasting, and public safety and security.

National Institute of Telecommunications

National Institute of Telecommunications ( INT ) is the former name of the Évry campus of the Institut Mines-Télécom common to the two Grandes Ecoles : Télécom SudParis , an engineering school , and Institut Mines-Télécom Business School , a management school . This name no longer has any place since decree no. 2012-279 of theFebruary 28, 2012relating to the Institut Mines-Télécom (IMT).

List of telecommunications/ICT regulatory authorities

The List of telecommunications regulatory authorities gives the official names and websites of the bodies responsible for regulating telecommunications (and more generally ICT) for most countries in the world. Each of these websites presents information on the implementation of the regulations and on the characteristics of the use of ICT in the country concerned.
